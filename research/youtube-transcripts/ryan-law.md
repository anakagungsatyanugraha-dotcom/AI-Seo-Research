## Transcript Summary

Ryan Law explains the AI-assisted editorial workflow used by the Ahrefs content team to produce high-quality SEO articles. Rather than asking AI to generate complete articles in a single prompt, the workflow breaks content creation into multiple structured stages, including content briefs, outlining, drafting, editing, internal linking, metadata generation, and WordPress publishing.

The process begins with a manually created content brief that defines the target keyword, working title, key ideas, and business objectives. AI then generates a structured outline based on these requirements before drafting the article. Ryan reviews every stage, providing editorial feedback and refining the output before moving to the next step.

Throughout the discussion, Ryan emphasizes that AI should support experienced marketers rather than replace them. Human editors remain responsible for reviewing facts, adding original insights, sharing first-hand experience, ensuring information gain, and maintaining editorial quality. The speakers also discuss limitations of AI, including hallucinated links, fabricated experiences, and the need for careful human verification before publication.

The workflow demonstrates how AI can automate repetitive writing tasks while allowing marketers to focus on strategy, expertise, and producing genuinely valuable content.

## Key Takeaways

- AI performs best when content production follows a structured workflow rather than a single prompt.
- Human-created content briefs guide AI toward higher-quality outputs.
- Editorial review remains essential for accuracy, originality, and quality.
- AI should assist writers, not replace subject-matter expertise.
- Information gain and unique insights remain important for SEO success.
- AI-generated content should always be reviewed before publication.
- AI can accelerate outlining, drafting, editing, metadata creation, and publishing workflows.
