## Transcript Summary

Michael King presents a comprehensive framework for understanding how AI-powered search systems fundamentally differ from traditional search engines. He argues that SEO is evolving into **Generative Engine Optimization (GEO)**, where success depends on optimizing content for AI retrieval, reasoning, and synthesis rather than solely for keyword rankings.

A central concept is **Retrieval-Augmented Generation (RAG)**, where AI combines large language models with search systems to retrieve relevant passages before generating responses. King explains that AI search relies heavily on semantic retrieval, vector embeddings, query expansion, and synthetic queries to identify content that best satisfies user intent.

He introduces **Relevance Engineering**, a methodology that integrates technical SEO, AI, information retrieval, content strategy, digital PR, user experience, and data science. Instead of relying on traditional SEO checklists, marketers should build content that AI systems can easily retrieve, verify, and extract.

King also demonstrates how AI evaluates content using passage-level relevance rather than entire pages. He recommends creating highly structured, evidence-rich content with clear headings, concise paragraphs, semantic triples, original data, and explicit factual statements. These characteristics improve extractability and increase the likelihood of being cited in AI-generated responses.

The presentation further explores AI Search measurement, recommending that marketers track AI visibility, citation frequency, synthetic query coverage, and business outcomes rather than relying solely on keyword rankings or organic traffic. Throughout the session, King emphasizes experimentation, statistical validation, and continuous optimization as AI search systems continue to evolve.
