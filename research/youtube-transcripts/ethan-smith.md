## Transcript Summary

Ethan Smith explains how Answer Engine Optimization (AEO) is evolving alongside traditional SEO as AI-powered search engines such as ChatGPT, Gemini, and Google AI Overviews increasingly retrieve and summarize web content. Drawing on client case studies from Webflow, Rippling, and Legit Security, he argues that many established SEO practices remain effective because AI search relies heavily on Retrieval-Augmented Generation (RAG), where large language models first retrieve search results before generating answers.

A central theme is that businesses should not abandon SEO. Instead, Ethan recommends extending existing SEO strategies by optimizing content for AI retrieval while expanding visibility across multiple citation sources, including owned websites, YouTube, Reddit, affiliate sites, and help centers. AI systems frequently synthesize information from these different channels when generating responses.

Ethan also demonstrates how AI search differs from traditional search through conversational follow-up questions and longer search journeys. Because of this, companies should create content that answers specific product features, integrations, use cases, and customer questions rather than focusing only on high-volume informational keywords. Help centers, product documentation, and support articles become valuable AI citation sources.

The presentation challenges several common assumptions about AEO. Ethan argues that many popular recommendations—such as LLM.txt files or excessive focus on Core Web Vitals—currently have little evidence supporting their impact on AI visibility. Instead, he encourages marketers to validate strategies through experimentation rather than following industry hype.

Finally, Ethan discusses the growing importance of authentic participation on platforms such as Reddit, high-quality YouTube content, schema markup, and first-party expertise. He predicts that future AI agents will increasingly perform actions on behalf of users, making structured product information and trustworthy content even more important.

## Key Takeaways

- Traditional SEO continues to support AI Search because RAG retrieves search results before generating answers.
- AI visibility should be built across owned websites, YouTube, Reddit, affiliate sites, and product documentation.
- Help centers and product documentation are valuable sources for AI citations.
- B2B companies should create educational YouTube videos around customer problems, not only product demonstrations.
- Authentic participation on Reddit is more effective than promotional spam.
- Schema markup continues to provide measurable value, while LLM.txt currently lacks strong evidence of effectiveness.
- Core Web Vitals have limited impact compared with content quality and relevance.
- AI Search favors comprehensive pages that answer related follow-up questions.
- Information Gain, original insights, and personal experience strengthen AI visibility.
- Marketers should test AEO strategies experimentally instead of relying on unverified best practices.
