## Post 1

**Date:** Approximately 1 week ago

**Topic:** New Google Search Console social reporting features

### Summary

Lily Ray discusses new social reporting features in Google Search Console, particularly for X (formerly Twitter). The update allows marketers to identify which search queries generate impressions and clicks for social posts, determine which posts appear in Google Search, Google Discover, and Google News, and better understand how social content contributes to overall search visibility.

She also notes that while the feature is useful, it may distract from broader concerns such as declining organic search traffic and the lack of AI-related reporting within Google Search Console.

### Key Takeaways

- Google Search Console now reports search performance for social posts.
- Users can identify which search queries trigger impressions and clicks.
- Performance data is available for Google Search, Discover, and Google News.
- Social content can contribute to organic search visibility.
- Current GSC still lacks detailed reporting for AI search features.

### Why it matters for AI-powered SEO content production

This post demonstrates how SEO professionals increasingly evaluate content across multiple discovery channels rather than relying solely on traditional website traffic. As AI search and social search continue to converge, understanding how social content appears in search results helps B2B SaaS marketers build more comprehensive content distribution and optimization strategies.

## Post 2

**Date:** Approximately 2 days ago

**Topic:** Google Search Console introduces Platform Properties

### Summary

Lily Ray highlights Google's announcement of the new Platform Properties feature in Google Search Console. The update enables creators and businesses to measure how content published on platforms such as Instagram, TikTok, X, and YouTube performs within Google Search and Google Discover.

She describes this as further evidence that SEO is evolving into "search everywhere optimization," where marketers must optimize and measure visibility across multiple platforms instead of focusing only on traditional websites.

### Key Takeaways

- Google Search Console now supports Platform Properties for social platforms.
- Users can track impressions, clicks, and search queries for social content.
- Performance data includes Google Search and Google Discover.
- SEO measurement is expanding beyond websites into social ecosystems.
- Multi-platform visibility is becoming increasingly important.

### Why it matters for AI-powered SEO content production

AI search systems increasingly combine information from websites, social platforms, videos, and other online sources. This update reinforces that successful B2B SaaS SEO requires optimizing content across multiple channels and measuring performance beyond traditional website rankings. It aligns with the broader trend toward "search everywhere optimization."
