## Post 1

**Date:** Approximately 3 weeks ago

**Topic:** AI search clicks differ between B2B and B2C content

### Summary

Ross Hudgens shares findings from Siege Media's analysis of 61 brands, comparing how AI search drives traffic to different content types across B2B and B2C companies. The study found that editorial content generates significantly more AI search clicks for B2B companies than for B2C brands.

He explains that B2B companies benefit from publishing bottom-of-funnel (BOFU) content such as "Best X" and comparison pages, which are frequently cited by large language models. In contrast, B2C purchase-intent content is often dominated by affiliate websites and large publishers, making product pages and affiliate partnerships more important for AI visibility.

### Key Takeaways

- AI search behaves differently across B2B and B2C industries.
- B2B editorial content receives substantially more AI-driven traffic.
- "Best X" and comparison pages are frequently cited by AI systems.
- B2C brands should strengthen product pages and affiliate relationships.
- AI search strategy should be adapted to each industry's search landscape.

### Why it matters for AI-powered SEO content production

This research demonstrates that B2B SaaS companies should prioritize high-quality comparison pages, buyer guides, and decision-stage content because these assets are more likely to be cited by AI search systems and influence purchasing decisions.

## Post 2

**Date:** Approximately 1 week ago

**Topic:** YouTube videos remain valuable for AI search citations

### Summary

Ross Hudgens analyzes 366 YouTube videos appearing in transactional AI search prompts such as "What is the best X?" He found that the average cited video is approximately nine months old, suggesting that valuable video content has a long lifespan within AI search systems.

The research also shows that third-party creators and affiliates produce most of the cited videos. Although YouTube may generate relatively few direct clicks, AI search systems frequently reference video descriptions and creator recommendations, making video an important long-term asset for search visibility.

### Key Takeaways

- AI search frequently cites YouTube content for commercial queries.
- Videos remain valuable for nearly a year after publication.
- Third-party creators dominate AI search video citations.
- Video descriptions influence AI retrieval and recommendations.
- Investing in expert video content can improve long-term AI visibility.

### Why it matters for AI-powered SEO content production

The findings suggest that B2B SaaS companies should treat YouTube as an important component of AI search optimization. High-quality educational videos and partnerships with trusted creators can increase the likelihood of being cited by AI systems, even when direct video traffic is relatively small.
