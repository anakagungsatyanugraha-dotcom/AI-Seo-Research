## Post 1

**Date:** Approximately 1 week ago

**Topic:** AI Search Collapse – What happens if AI-generated content ranks in AI?

### Summary

Ethan Smith shares research conducted with Graphite and Axios examining how AI search systems behave when AI-generated content begins citing other AI-generated content. Using over one million LLM API calls across three large language models and more than 1,000 prompts, the study found that AI responses become increasingly homogeneous when AI-generated content enters retrieval systems.

The research showed that even a small proportion of AI-generated references can significantly reduce answer diversity because language models preferentially cite AI-generated content. This creates a risk of "AI collapse," where multiple independent answers eventually converge into nearly identical responses.

### Key Takeaways

- AI-generated content can reduce the diversity of AI search results.
- The study analyzed more than one million API calls across three LLMs.
- AI systems were found to prefer AI-generated references over human-written sources.
- Even a small number of AI-generated references can trigger answer collapse.
- Original, human-created content remains critical for maintaining diverse and reliable AI search results.

### Why it matters for AI-powered SEO content production

The study highlights the importance of publishing original, experience-based content rather than mass-producing AI-generated articles. For B2B SaaS companies, authentic expertise and first-party research are more likely to remain valuable as AI search evolves.

## Post 2

**Date:** Approximately 1 week ago

**Topic:** Measuring AI Search (AEO) conversions using self-reported attribution

### Summary

Ethan Smith presents a case study conducted with n8n comparing traditional last-touch attribution in Google Analytics (GA4) with self-reported "How Did You Hear About Us?" (HDYHAU) surveys. The results showed that AI search contributes significantly more conversions than GA4 reports because many users discover brands through AI assistants but later return via direct visits or branded searches.

The study found that AI search conversions were approximately ten times higher when measured using self-reported attribution instead of last-touch analytics. Ethan argues that businesses should combine analytics with customer surveys to better understand the true business impact of AI search and SEO.

### Key Takeaways

- Last-touch attribution underestimates AI search conversions.
- Many AI-assisted customer journeys are "zero-click."
- Self-reported attribution provides a more complete picture of marketing performance.
- SEO and AI search often influence customers earlier in the buying journey.
- Combining analytics with survey data improves marketing measurement.

### Why it matters for AI-powered SEO content production

As AI assistants increasingly influence purchasing decisions without generating direct website clicks, B2B SaaS marketers need new attribution models to measure the real value of AI visibility. Understanding AI-assisted customer journeys helps justify investments in AI search optimization and content strategy.
