## Post 1

**Date:** Approximately 1 week ago

**Topic:** AI search attribution versus Google Analytics

### Summary

Thomas Peham shares OtterlyAI's June conversion data to demonstrate how traditional analytics underreport traffic and conversions generated by AI assistants such as Claude and ChatGPT. While Google Analytics attributes only 0.1% of signups to Claude, self-reported attribution shows Claude contributing 10.6% of conversions and ChatGPT contributing 11%. He argues that AI assistants are becoming important discovery channels even though standard analytics tools fail to capture their true impact.

### Key Takeaways

- Google Analytics significantly underestimates AI-assisted customer acquisition.
- Self-reported attribution provides a more complete picture of AI search influence.
- Claude and ChatGPT function as brand discovery channels rather than simple referral sources.
- AI visitors demonstrate high purchase intent and strong conversion rates.
- Businesses should optimize for Google Search, ChatGPT, and Claude simultaneously.

### Why it matters for AI-powered SEO content production

This post illustrates how AI search is changing customer journeys. As more users discover brands through large language models instead of traditional search engines, B2B SaaS companies need to optimize content for AI visibility while adopting better attribution methods to measure AI-driven conversions.

## Post 2

**Date:** Approximately 1 week ago

**Topic:** Claude Citation Study — What Claude actually cites

### Summary

Thomas Peham shares findings from OtterlyAI's Claude Citation Study, highlighting which sources Claude most frequently cites when generating responses. The study found that Reddit (0%), social media (0.9%), and YouTube (0.3%) receive very few citations, while authoritative brand websites are cited far more often. He concludes that companies investing in high-quality website content are well positioned for visibility in AI-generated search results.

### Key Takeaways

- Claude rarely cites Reddit, social media, or YouTube content.
- Brand websites are the primary sources referenced by Claude.
- High-quality website content is more valuable than relying solely on social platforms.
- AI search optimization increasingly depends on building authoritative web content.

### Why it matters for AI-powered SEO content production

This post provides practical evidence for AI search optimization strategies. It suggests that B2B SaaS companies should prioritize publishing well-structured, authoritative content on their own websites, as these sources are significantly more likely to be cited by AI assistants such as Claude than social media or community platforms.
